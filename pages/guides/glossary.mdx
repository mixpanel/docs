## Product & User Lifecycle Terms
<details>
<summary>View terms</summary>

### Active User
People who currently use your product (distinct from just "Users").

### New User
Someone who was first identified within a specified period.

### Dormant User
A user who did not complete a specified action within the current period but had completed it in the previous period.

### Bounce Rate
The percentage of sessions where users had only one pageview, no autocaptures, and spent less than 10 seconds on the page.

</details>

## Data & Technical Terms
<details>
<summary>View terms</summary>

### Alias
A related user account used to connect two user accounts and their data.

### Autocapture
The ability to capture user behavior automatically without dedicated instrumentation.

### Identify
The process of collecting, connecting, and combining data to a user; de-anonymizing a user with properties.

### Ingestion
The process of getting events users trigger into the analytics platform.

### SDK (Software Development Kit)
Tools for building and using a software platform.

### First-party Cookies
Data packets served by the website operator to store user preferences.

### Third-party Cookies
Cookies served by external parties.

</details>

## Analysis & Visualization Terms
<details>
<summary>View terms</summary>

### Annotation
Short text notes applied to data to provide context.

### Dashboard
A collection of multiple insights saved in one place for collaboration.

### Formula
An equation or calculation that modifies data from one or more series.

### Stickiness
How many times users perform an action within a set period.

## Business Model Terms
<details>
<summary>View terms</summary>

### B2B (Business to Business)
Selling to other businesses rather than individual users.

### B2C (Business to Consumer)
Selling to individual customers rather than businesses.

### DAU (Daily Active Users)
How many active users you have each day.

### MOM (Month-on-Month)
Tracking performance from one month to the next.

### NPS (Net Promoter Score)
A metric for understanding customer satisfaction and loyalty.

### MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)
A potential customer identified as more likely to convert based on engagement signals.

</details>

Used to divide users into groups where a feature is enabled or disabled.

### MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
The simplest version of something built to test an idea.

An A/B test with more than two variants.

## Data Management Terms
<details>
<summary>View terms</summary>

### Data Lake
A system for storing raw data for ambiguous or unknown purposes.

### Data Warehouse
A system for storing processed data for specific, known purposes.

### Groups
Aggregating events by entities other than individual users (companies, teams, devices).

### Session
A period of time when users are engaged and interacting with your product.

</details>
